Introduction to Graphs

Welcome to the world of graph theory! In this tutorial, we'll explore the basics of graphs, including key definitions and fundamental concepts.

What is a Graph?

A graph is a mathematical structure used to model pairwise relations between objects. It consists of two sets:

  • Vertices (or Nodes): The fundamental units or points.
  • Edges (or Links): The connections between the vertices.

Formally, a graph is denoted as \( G = (V, E) \), where \( V \) is a set of vertices, and \( E \) is a set of edges connecting pairs of vertices.

Key Definitions

  • Adjacent Vertices: Two vertices are adjacent if they are connected by an edge.
  • Degree of a Vertex: The number of edges incident to a vertex.
  • Path: A sequence of edges that connects a sequence of vertices.
  • Cycle: A path that starts and ends at the same vertex without repeating any edges or vertices (except the starting/ending vertex).

Applications of Graphs

Graphs are used in various fields:

  • Computer Networks: Modeling connections between computers.
  • Social Networks: Representing relationships between people.
  • Transportation: Mapping routes and networks.
  • Biology: Analyzing neural networks or ecological relationships.
  • Even Sudoku!
