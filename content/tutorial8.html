Planarity and Graph Drawing

A graph is planar if it can be drawn on a plane without any edges crossing.

Euler's Formula

For any connected planar graph:

\[ V - E + F = 2 \]

Where:

  • \( V \) = number of vertices
  • \( E \) = number of edges
  • \( F \) = number of faces (including the outer, infinite face)

Kuratowski's Theorem

A finite graph is planar if and only if it does not contain a subgraph that is a subdivision of \( K_5 \) (complete graph of 5 vertices) or \( K_{3,3} \) (complete bipartite graph of two sets of 3 vertices).
