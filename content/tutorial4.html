Graph Traversals

Traversing a graph involves visiting all the vertices and edges systematically. Two fundamental traversal algorithms are Breadth-First Search (BFS) and Depth-First Search (DFS).

Breadth-First Search (BFS)

BFS explores the graph level by level. It starts at a specified root vertex and explores all its neighbors before moving to the next level of vertices.

Algorithm Steps

  1. Enqueue the root node and mark it as visited.
  2. While the queue is not empty:
    1. Dequeue a vertex \( v \).
    2. For each unvisited neighbor \( w \) of \( v \):
      1. Mark \( w \) as visited.
      2. Enqueue \( w \).

Depth-First Search (DFS)

DFS explores as far as possible along each branch before backtracking. It uses a stack (often via recursion) to keep track of the path.

Algorithm Steps

  1. Start at the root node and mark it as visited.
  2. For each unvisited neighbor \( w \) of the current vertex \( v \):
    1. Recursively perform DFS on \( w \).
